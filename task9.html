Early warning system - Loan Asset Management

Institutional Lending (Commercial Loan) is governed by Commercial loan agreement. These agreements contain the terms of agreement between the Bank (Lender) and the client (Corporates) Ref - https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/922204/000119312509149119/dex1038.htm All agreements have clauses within the agreements called Covenants. The covenants are legally binding terms that enable the lender to ensure compliance with the terms of the agreement. Any failure by the borrower to comply with the terms of the covenants could trigger compensatory or legal action. A few examples of Covenants could be: Maintenance of Financial ratios - say Debt to Asset ratio Limit on Dividend payout during the tenure of the loan Retention of key employees in the corporate Maintenance of adequate insurance The breakage of a Covenant could trigger different type of actions by the lender 1. Call back the obligation 2. Measures to reduce Risk The above necessitates the understanding of Covenants and capture of the critical data elements required to monitor compliance with the covenants, triggers to highlight breakages of the covenants, tracking next steps with regard to the non compliance, etc....
